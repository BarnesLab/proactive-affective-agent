Cancer survivors complete ecological momentary assessments (EMA) several times per day, reporting their emotional state (positive affect, negative affect, discrete emotions). Meanwhile, their smartphones passively collect sensing data (screen usage, motion, keyboard, ambient light, music).


Can an LLM predict emotional states from passive sensing data alone? And does giving it access to diary narratives and autonomous tool-use improve predictions?

Key Findings

✓
V4 (agentic multimodal) is the best binary classifier
BA=0.670, F1=0.556 — leads across nearly all binary metrics. Beats the AR baseline (BA=0.658). Autonomous tool-use + diary access is the winning combination for discrete emotion classification.
✓
CALLM has the best continuous predictions (MAE)
Mean MAE=3.528, best PA calibration (pred 18.5 vs GT 18.8). Diary-only with TF-IDF RAG produces well-calibrated continuous scores, but weaker binary classification (BA=0.615).
✓
Agentic > Structured (consistent pattern)
V2 (agentic) beats V1 (structured) in sensing-only: BA 0.596 vs 0.534, F1 0.477 vs 0.292.
V4 (agentic) beats V3 (structured) in multimodal: BA 0.670 vs 0.617, F1 0.556 vs 0.472.
Autonomous data querying outperforms fixed-pipeline feature engineering.
✓
Multimodal > Sensing-only (consistent pattern)
V3 beats V1 (structured): BA 0.617 vs 0.534, MAE 3.97 vs 6.85.
V4 beats V2 (agentic): BA 0.670 vs 0.596, F1 0.556 vs 0.477.
Diary narratives add substantial predictive signal beyond passive sensing alone.
⚠
Sensing-only agents overestimate negative affect
V1: NA_MAE=9.91, V2: NA_MAE=9.42 (GT mean is only 1.7). Without diary context, the LLM defaults to predicting high distress from passive sensing patterns. Multimodal versions (V3: 4.32, CALLM: 3.68) are much better calibrated.
⚠
All versions under-predict variance
GT SD for PA is 8.2-8.3, but predictions range from SD=2.8 (V1) to SD=5.3 (CALLM). LLMs tend to regress toward the mean, especially without diary context.
